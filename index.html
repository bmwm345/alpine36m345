My account of the maintenance performed on my 1998 M3/4/5.

The E36 is the direct descendant of two elite bloodlines. The E30 M3 gave it its identity, the reason the M3 nameplate exists at all today. The E34 M5 gave it its purpose. When BMW retired the E34 in 1995 and the E39 wasn't ready, the E36 M3 was made a sedan as an additional option for a couple of years to carry the entire ///M executive lineage

To their surprise, the E36 did way better than expected in the US market following the E30 flop
