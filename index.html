My account of the maintenance performed on my 1998 M3/4/5. Yes, it has four doors. No, it's not a "family car." In 1995, following the retirement of the E34 M5, BMW faced a void in their high-performance executive lineup. To bridge the gap between the E34 and the next-generation E39 M5, BMW needed a vehicle capable of filling the E34's shoes while restoring the reputation of both the M3 and the ///M division. Despite heavy investment in the US during the 1980s, the E30 M3 had not sold well. Consequently, BMW detuned the North American E36 M3 so that if it failed to capture the market like its predecessor, it wouldn't "dig another deep grave" for the brand. As a result, BMW ///M gave the E36 M3 two extra doors and a bit of dignity. To their surprise, the E36 M3 sold exceedingly well; it is the primary reason the current BMW ///M lineup is so palatable to the global market today. So yes, this car is the reason why BMW ///M is still a thing today.
